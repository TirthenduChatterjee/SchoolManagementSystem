About Us

Teiko International High School is located in Ota, a ward of the Tokyo Metropolis that literally means "Big Rice Field" and it is the largest of Tokyo’s 23 wards. It is home to Haneda Airport, Tokyo’s international airport and as well as some of the world’s leading manufacturing companies. It also has bustling shopping streets and green residential areas.

Historic Priviledge

Throughout history, schools have been a bastion of privilege, serving select segments of society while excluding others. In ancient civilizations, education was reserved for the elite and privileged classes, reinforcing social hierarchies and limiting opportunities for the masses. The Renaissance and Enlightenment brought about some progress in advocating for broader access to education, emphasizing the importance of knowledge for personal development. However, it wasn't until the Industrial Revolution that the concept of public education began to take shape, marking a significant shift toward a more inclusive approach. Despite these advancements, issues of privilege persisted, with funding disparities and segregation perpetuating unequal opportunities for various social groups. The fight for equal access to education continued throughout the 20th century, with civil rights movements striving to dismantle legal barriers and promote inclusivity. While strides have been made, the historical legacy of privilege in schools reminds us of the importance of ongoing efforts to ensure education is a universal right for all.
